# Plan 03-04: Monitor Pipeline and Admin Approval Integration

## Goal

Wire the summary and notification components into the monitor/admin flow so new articles produce summaries and WhatsApp alerts, while seeded or changed known posts become approval-needed instead of triggering unapproved AI calls or duplicate alerts.

## Scope

This plan integrates Phase 3 behavior into `MonitorRunService`, admin DTOs, and tests. It does not add scheduling, Slack, digest mode, or a full dashboard.

## User Story

As the Airwallex FYI operator, I want `/admin/run-once` to process new public Airwallex posts into AI summaries and WhatsApp alerts, while clearly showing anything that needs my permission before summarization.

## Artifacts This Phase Produces

- New-post end-to-end summary and alert orchestration.
- Approval-needed classification for seeded/missing-summary and content-changed known posts.
- Manual protected admin endpoint for approving one post summary without duplicate alert sends.
- Extended `MonitorRunResult` and admin DTOs with summary/alert counts, payload previews, and sampled errors.
- End-to-end service and MockMvc tests covering dry-run, summary failures, Twilio failures, approval-needed status, and security.

## Must Haves

### Truths

- D-06: Newly discovered `DISCOVERED` posts auto-summarize.
- D-07: Seeded/history posts missing summaries do not auto-call AI; they surface as approval-needed.
- D-08: Known URL content changes do not auto-call AI; they surface as approval-needed.
- D-09: Changed known URLs do not create duplicate alerts.
- D-10: Keep approval lightweight; a small protected admin action is allowed.
- D-14: Dry-run uses Gemini and builds/saves payload but skips Twilio.
- D-15: Dry-run must never call Twilio.
- D-17: Twilio failure is recorded and surfaced without retry.
- D-20: `/admin/run-once` returns counts plus bounded samples.
- D-21: `/admin/run-once` avoids unbounded summaries/payloads/errors.
- AI-01, NOTIF-01, and NOTIF-02 all need observable tests in this final integration slice.

### Constraints

- Preserve first-run seed behavior and no historical spam.
- Preserve existing admin token protection for all admin endpoints.
- Keep endpoint responses bounded.
- Do not add scheduler or command-line run-once behavior in this phase.

## Threat Model

<threat_model>
- Risk: seeded/history posts create AI cost or spam. Mitigation: only `PostApplyKind.NEW` auto-calls summarizer/notifier; seeded and changed known posts become approval-needed.
- Risk: changed known URL creates duplicate alert. Mitigation: changed known URLs never call notifier automatically and notification attempts remain unique by post/channel/recipient.
- Risk: manual approval endpoint becomes an unauthenticated AI trigger. Mitigation: reuse existing admin token filter and add security tests.
- Risk: dry-run appears safe but still calls Twilio. Mitigation: result has `twilioCallsTriggered`, dry-run notifier has no Twilio dependency, and tests assert false.
- Risk: admin response leaks too much provider/output data. Mitigation: bounded payload previews and sampled errors only.
</threat_model>
